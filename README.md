# Quadruped-Robot-Mechanical-Design
This repository contains the preliminary mechanical design and analysis for a quadruped robot. The project focuses on the fundamental mechanical principles required for robotic locomotion.
#Project Overview
The objective is to design a basic quadruped robot capable of standing and walking. The design emphasizes mechanical stability and joint kinematics.
#Design Specifications
 Body Structure: Minimalist chassis designed for optimal weight distribution.
 Leg Design: Four-legged configuration, each featuring three joints to allow for natural movement.
 Degrees of Freedom (DoF): The robot utilizes a total of 12 degrees of freedom (3 DoF per leg).
 Actuators: Servo-based actuation for precise joint control.
 Mechanical Analysis:
 Torque Calculation: Preliminary torque analysis performed for individual joints.
 Stability & Center of Gravity: Analysis focused on maintaining a low center of gravity to ensure static stability.
 Locomotion Method: Proposed gait patterns for stable walking.
 Technical Challenges: Potential mechanical constraints and expected operational limitations identified.
